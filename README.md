# WDAI AI Business Toolkit

Built by Patty O'Brien Novak and Anennya for the [Women Defining AI](https://womendefinigai.com) community.

A set of Claude Code slash commands that walk members through starting and running an AI business — consulting, automation workflows, or apps. Each command produces real, actionable business artifacts tailored to the member's specific situation. No generic advice.

---

## How to Use

These commands run inside [Claude Code](https://claude.ai/code). Type any command to get started — no arguments needed, each one will ask you what it needs to know.

---

## Commands

### `/discover` — Find Your Niche + Ideal Client
*Maps to: Phase 1, Items 1 + 2 — Pick a Niche, Positioning & Messaging*

Guides you through 4 questions to surface your background, the pain points you've witnessed, who you want to work with, and where AI fits. Synthesizes your answers into a one-page **Niche + ICP Snapshot** including:

- **Niche statement** — one sentence describing who you serve and what problem you solve
- **Ideal Client Profile (ICP)** — Who, the trigger that makes them need you now, and why the timing is right
- **Competitive snapshot** — what alternatives your client has today and the gap you fill
- **Draft positioning line** — your one-sentence offer, outcome-first

Ends with a "Now share it" nudge and a handoff to `/validate`.

---

### `/ceo-define-problem` — Define the Problem Worth Solving
*The foundation step — based on Jobs to Be Done. Run this first.*

Walks you through 4 questions — who you're solving for, the moment the problem shows up, what they do about it today, and what "solved" looks like — then synthesizes a one-page **Problem Statement**:

- **The core problem** in one sentence — *"When [situation], your customer wants to [motivation], so they can [outcome]"*
- **What "solved" looks like** across functional, emotional, and social layers
- **Who has the problem** (your customer seed) and **what they do today** (your real competition — usually not another AI tool)
- **A one-line niche** seed

Saves to your shared business brief so later commands inherit the context. Ends with a "Now share it" nudge and a handoff to `/cpo-define` — *define the problem, then define the product that solves it.*
