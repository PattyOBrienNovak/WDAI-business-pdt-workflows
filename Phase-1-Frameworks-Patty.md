## 1. Validate Before You Build

### Framework: The 3-Filter + 10-Conversation Test

**The 3 Filters (run every idea through all three before going further):**

| Filter | Question | Pass = |
|---|---|---|
| Frequency | Does this problem happen at least weekly for the client? | Yes |
| Cost | Does it cost the business $50K+ per year (time, lost revenue, errors)? | Yes |
| Budget authority | Is there someone who can approve spending within 30 days? | Yes |

If any filter fails, either sharpen the idea or move on.

**The 10-Conversation Framework:**

Before building anything, have 10 real conversations with potential clients. Here's the structure for each:

1. **Open:** "I'm exploring whether [problem] is something businesses like yours struggle with. Can I ask you a few questions?"
2. **Pain discovery:** "Walk me through what happens when [problem] occurs. What does it cost you?"
3. **Current solution:** "What are you doing about it today? What's frustrating about that?"
4. **Desire:** "If this were solved, what would that make possible?"
5. **The money question:** "If someone could solve this for you reliably, is that something you'd invest in?"

After 10 conversations, look for patterns. If at least 7 people described the same pain and said yes to question 5, you have something real.

**The Currency Test:**
> "I'm building something to solve exactly this. I'm looking for 3 founding clients to shape it with me — at a reduced rate in exchange for feedback. Would you be one of them?"

If they say yes and pull out a credit card (or ask for an invoice), you've validated. If they say "sounds great, let me know when it's ready" — that's not validation.

## 2. Pick a Niche

### Framework: The Niche Selection Matrix

Score each potential niche on these 5 dimensions (1–5 each, 25 = perfect):

| Dimension | Question | Your Score (1–5) |
|---|---|---|
| Your knowledge | Do you already know this industry or problem? | |
| Pain intensity | How expensive or urgent is this problem for them? | |
| Budget | Do they have money to spend on solutions? | |
| Competition | Is this underserved or overrun with AI consultants? | |
| Access | Do you have a way to reach these people? | |

**Total: ___/25**

Anything above 18 is worth pursuing. Below 12, keep looking.

**The Niche Statement Formula:**

Fill in the blanks:
> "I help **[specific type of business]** that are struggling with **[specific problem]** use AI to **[specific outcome]** — without **[the thing they fear most]**."

Examples:
- "I help independent real estate agencies that are drowning in repetitive admin use AI to handle their listing workflows — without hiring more staff."
- "I help e-commerce brands struggling to produce enough content use AI to build a content engine — without losing their brand voice."

Write 3 versions. The one that feels slightly scary because it's so specific is usually the right one.

**The Beachhead Rule:**
Start with the smallest, most specific version of your niche. Dominate that first. Expand later. "AI for small businesses" is not a niche. "AI operations tools for solo HR consultants in the US" is.

## 4. Set Up Your Business Structure

### Framework: The Business Setup Checklist

Do these in order. Each one unlocks the next.

**Week 1 — The Essentials (do these before your first client call)**
- [ ] Decide: LLC or sole proprietor? (LLC recommended — see research file)
- [ ] If LLC: file with your state (legalzoom.com or your state's business portal — $100–$500)
- [ ] Get your EIN: IRS.gov → "Apply for an EIN Online" → takes 10 minutes, free
- [ ] Open a dedicated business checking account (bring your EIN + LLC docs)
- [ ] Set up a business email address (yourname@yourbusiness.com via Google Workspace ~$6/mo)

**Week 2 — The Basics**
- [ ] Get a simple invoicing tool (Wave is free; start there)
- [ ] Create a folder system for client files (Google Drive or Notion)
- [ ] Set up a separate savings account for taxes — move 25–30% of every payment here immediately
- [ ] Find a simple service agreement template (see Item 8 below)

**Week 3 — The Nice-to-Haves**
- [ ] Set up a scheduling link (Calendly free tier)
- [ ] Create a simple one-page website or LinkedIn profile that explains what you do
- [ ] Find a CPA or accountant who works with freelancers/consultants

**The One Non-Negotiable:**
Separate business and personal money from day one. This single habit saves enormous pain at tax time and is required if you ever want to prove your LLC is legitimate.

## 5. Price Your Services

### Framework: The Pricing Ladder

Build your pricing in tiers so every client has a natural entry point and a next step:

```
TIER 1 — THE ENTRY OFFER (easiest yes)
Name: [Your Diagnostic / AI Audit / etc.]
What it is: A fixed-scope assessment of [specific problem area]
What they get: [2-page report / 60-min debrief / roadmap / etc.]
Price: $1,500–$2,500
Time to deliver: 3–5 business days
Who it's for: Anyone considering working with you; leads naturally to Tier 2

TIER 2 — THE CORE OFFER (your main service)
Name: ___
What it is: ___
What they get: ___
Price: $___
Time to deliver: ___
Who it's for: Clients who did Tier 1 OR who come in ready to buy

TIER 3 — ONGOING / RETAINER (recurring revenue)
Name: ___
What it is: Monthly support / maintenance / advisory
What they get: ___ hours/month + [specific deliverables]
Price: $___ /month
Who it's for: Clients who completed Tier 2 and want continued support
```

**The Value-Based Pricing Formula:**

1. Identify the cost of the problem you solve: $___/year
2. Estimate the value of solving it (revenue gained or cost saved): $___/year
3. Charge 10–20% of that value

> Example: Client wastes $120,000/year on manual data entry your workflow eliminates → charge $12,000–$24,000 for the project.

**The Pricing Sanity Check:**
Say your price out loud. If you want to apologize after saying it, it's probably the right price. If you feel nothing, charge more.

## 6. Get Your First Clients

### Framework: The Warm-to-Cold Progression

Don't skip steps. Go in order.

**Stage 1: Your Existing Network (Week 1–2)**

Make a list of 50 people you already know. For each one, ask:
- Do they need what I offer?
- Do they know someone who does?

Script for reaching out:
> "Hey [Name] — I'm launching [what you do] for [who you serve]. I'm looking for my first few clients and thought of you. Are you dealing with [problem] in your work? Or do you know someone who is? Happy to jump on a 20-minute call."

**Stage 2: LinkedIn Outreach (Week 2–4)**

1. Search LinkedIn using your ICP criteria
2. Connect with a personalized note (mention something specific about their work — not a pitch)
3. After they accept, send a value-first message — share something useful, ask a question
4. After 2–3 exchanges, ask if they'd like to talk

**Stage 3: Community Presence (Ongoing)**

Post in places your ideal clients already hang out — LinkedIn, industry Slack groups, association forums. Share observations, ask questions, answer others. Don't pitch. Let curiosity create inbound.

**Stage 4: Cold Email (Month 2+)**

Only after you have 1–2 clients and a case study. Cold email requires:
- A dedicated sending domain (not your main email)
- A tested subject line and opening line
- 100+ sends per week to see results
- A clear call to action (a 20-min call, not "let me know if you're interested")

**The First Client Checklist:**
- [ ] List of 50 warm contacts made
- [ ] 10 outreach messages sent
- [ ] 3 conversations scheduled
- [ ] Entry offer (Tier 1) ready to describe
- [ ] Calendar link ready to share

## 7. Build Your Delivery System

### Framework: The Client Project Lifecycle

Build these templates once, use them forever.

**Phase 1 — Before Work Starts**
- [ ] Discovery call completed (use the ICP questions from Item 3)
- [ ] Scope of work written and signed (see template below)
- [ ] 50% deposit invoiced and received
- [ ] Client added to project management tool
- [ ] Onboarding doc sent (what you need from them, timeline, how to reach you)

**Phase 2 — During the Work**
- [ ] Weekly status update sent (3 sentences: what happened, what's next, any blockers)
- [ ] Any scope changes documented and approved before doing the work
- [ ] Milestone 1 delivered and approved
- [ ] Milestone 2 delivered and approved

**Phase 3 — Delivery and Close**
- [ ] Final deliverable sent
- [ ] Debrief call scheduled
- [ ] Testimonial/case study request made (at peak satisfaction — during the debrief)
- [ ] Remaining invoice sent
- [ ] Upsell/next engagement planted (see Item 13)

## 8. Handle the Legal and Money Basics

### Framework: The Money & Legal Starter Kit

**The 5 Documents You Need (in order of urgency):**

1. **Service Agreement** — your master contract; covers the relationship
2. **Statement of Work (SOW)** — attached to the agreement for each project; covers scope
3. **Invoice template** — professional, includes payment terms and late fee language
4. **W-9** — required by US clients who pay you $600+ in a year (fill it out once, reuse)
5. **Change Request Form** — simple doc for anything outside original scope

**Invoice Template Structure:**

```
[Your Business Name]
[Address] | [Email] | [Phone]
EIN: ___

INVOICE #___
Date: ___
Due Date: ___ (Net 15 / Net 30)

Bill To:
[Client Name]
[Client Address]

SERVICES:
[Description of work].........................$___
[Description of work].........................$___

SUBTOTAL: $___
TAX (if applicable): $___
TOTAL DUE: $___

Payment methods: [Zelle / ACH / check / Stripe link]

Late payment: Invoices unpaid after [due date] are subject to a 1.5% monthly late fee.

Thank you for your business!
```

**Tax Rhythm:**
- Every payment received → move 25–30% to tax savings account immediately
- Quarterly estimated taxes due: April 15, June 15, September 15, January 15
- Track every business expense (software, home office, equipment, professional development)

**The "Getting Paid" Script (for overdue invoices):**

Day 1 after due date:
> "Hi [Name] — just a quick note that invoice #___ for $___ was due yesterday. Let me know if you need anything from me to process it."

Day 7:
> "Following up on invoice #___. Happy to resend or help with any vendor paperwork you need. Just let me know."

Day 14:
> "I want to make sure this doesn't fall through the cracks — invoice #___ is now 14 days past due. Can you give me a status update on when we can expect payment?"

## 9. Set Up Your Operations Stack

### Framework: The Stack Decision Guide

**Rule 1: Start with the minimum.** You don't need 10 tools. You need 4.

**The Non-Negotiable 4 (get these first):**
1. **Invoicing** — Wave (free) or FreshBooks (~$15/mo)
2. **Scheduling** — Calendly (free tier)
3. **Project tracking** — Notion (free) or ClickUp (free tier)
4. **Contracts** — HelloSign or DocuSign (~$15/mo) OR a Google Doc with e-signature

**Add these when you have 3+ clients:**
5. **CRM** — HubSpot free tier (track leads, follow-ups, pipeline)
6. **Accounting** — QuickBooks Online (~$30/mo, syncs with your bank)

**Add these when you're scaling:**
7. **Proposal software** — Better Proposals or Proposify
8. **Client portal** — to share files, updates, and deliverables in one place

**The Integration Principle:**
Connect tools at the minimum: Calendly → Google Calendar → your email. So a booked meeting automatically appears on your calendar and sends confirmation. That's it for now. Don't over-automate until you know your process is working.

**The "Do I Need This Tool?" Test:**
> "Does the absence of this tool cause me to lose money, miss follow-ups, or make mistakes?"
> If no → don't add it yet.

## 10. Position Yourself and Define Your Offer

### Framework: The Positioning Stack

Build your positioning from bottom to top. Each layer makes the next one easier.

```
LAYER 4 (TOP): Your tagline — 7 words or fewer
        ↑
LAYER 3: Your one-sentence offer
        ↑
LAYER 2: Your differentiator — why you, not someone else
        ↑
LAYER 1 (FOUNDATION): Your niche + ICP
```

**Layer 1 — Niche + ICP** (from Items 2 and 3):
> "I work with [niche] to solve [problem]."

**Layer 2 — Your Differentiator:**
Pick ONE. Just one.
- Industry depth: "I spent 10 years in [industry] before becoming an AI consultant"
- Process: "I use a 3-step diagnostic before any implementation — so we never build the wrong thing"
- Speed: "My clients have a working prototype in 2 weeks, not 6 months"
- Teaching: "I don't just build it — I make sure your team knows how to run it"
- Community: "I've built this alongside 200 WDAI members — this isn't theory"

**Layer 3 — One-Sentence Offer:**
> "I help [specific client] [achieve specific outcome] [using your method] — without [their biggest fear]."

Fill in:
- Specific client: ___
- Specific outcome: ___
- Your method: ___
- Their biggest fear: ___

**Layer 4 — Tagline (optional but useful for social bios):**
Examples:
- "AI that works for your business, not instead of you"
- "Making AI real for [niche]"
- "Your AI business partner, not your AI vendor"

**The Positioning Test:**
Say your Layer 3 sentence to 5 people in your target market. If at least 3 say "wait, how does that work?" — you've got it. If they nod politely and change the subject — rewrite it.

## 11. Promote Yourself Consistently

### Framework: The 1-to-5 Content Engine

One client win or work moment → 5 pieces of content. Do this every time.

```
SOURCE EVENT: [a client result, a lesson learned, a question you keep getting asked]
        ↓
1. LinkedIn post (3–5 sentences, insight + one takeaway)
        ↓
2. LinkedIn carousel or "here's what I did" thread
        ↓
3. Newsletter section (expand the post into 3 paragraphs)
        ↓
4. Short-form video script (60 seconds, same insight, talking head)
        ↓
5. A reusable framework or template (turn the lesson into a tool others can use)
```

**The 90-Day Consistency Plan:**

| Weeks | Focus | Output |
|---|---|---|
| 1–4 | Show up, share what you're learning | 3–5 posts/week, no pitching |
| 5–8 | Start sharing client work (anonymized if needed) | Add 1 framework/template per week |
| 9–12 | Introduce your offer naturally | 1 direct CTA post per week, rest still value |

**The "Building in Public" Post Formula:**

> "Here's something I discovered this week working with [type of client on type of problem]:"
> [The insight — 2–3 sentences]
> "What surprised me: [the non-obvious part]"
> "What I'd do differently: [the lesson]"
> "Anyone else seeing this?"

This format works because it's honest, specific, and ends with a question — which drives comments and reach.

**The Promotion Thread:**
Weave a "share it" moment into every business milestone:

| Milestone | What to share |
|---|---|
| Niche decision made | "Here's why I chose to focus on [niche]" |
| First client signed | "I just signed my first client. Here's how it happened." |
| Interesting discovery | "I expected X. Here's what actually happened." |
| Project delivered | "Here's what we built and what it's doing for the client" |
| Lesson learned hard | "I made this mistake so you don't have to" |

## 12. Manage Client Expectations

### Framework: The Expectations Conversation

Have this conversation at the START of every engagement — before any work begins.

**The 4-Part Kickoff Conversation:**

**Part 1 — Define success together:**
> "Before we start, I want to make sure we agree on what success looks like. In 90 days, what would have to be true for you to feel great about this investment?"
> Write down exactly what they say. Put it in the SOW.

**Part 2 — Address the AI reality:**
> "AI is genuinely powerful, and it also has real limits. Here's what it will do well for us: [specific]. Here's what it won't do: [specific]. I'd rather be honest now than explain surprises later."

**Part 3 — Set communication expectations:**
> "Here's how I work: you'll hear from me every [weekly/biweekly] with a brief update. If something comes up that changes our timeline or scope, I'll tell you immediately — not at the end. What's your preferred way to communicate?"

**Part 4 — Define the data/access you need:**
> "For this to work, I'll need access to [specific systems/data/people]. The #1 reason AI projects stall is not having clean data or the right access. Can we lock in what I need in the first week?"

**The Success Metrics Template:**

```
PROJECT: ___
We will know this project is working when:

Metric 1: [what we're measuring] moves from ___ to ___ by [date]
Metric 2: [what we're measuring] moves from ___ to ___ by [date]
Metric 3: [what we're measuring] moves from ___ to ___ by [date]

We will check these metrics at: [30 days / 60 days / 90 days]

If we're off track at 30 days, we will: [adjust X / add resource Y / escalate to Z]
```

## 13. Plan to Grow

### Framework: The Growth Flywheel

Each client, if handled well, creates the next one. Here's how to build that loop deliberately.

```
[Do great work]
        ↓
[Capture it as a case study]
        ↓
[Ask for a referral at peak happiness]
        ↓
[Turn the case study into content]
        ↓
[Plant the upsell before the project ends]
        ↓
[New client or expanded engagement]
        ↓
[repeat]
```

**The Case Study Template (3 sentences minimum):**

```
CLIENT TYPE: [industry, size — anonymize if needed]
PROBLEM: [What they were struggling with before you arrived]
APPROACH: [What you did — in plain language, no jargon]
RESULT: [What changed — specific numbers whenever possible]
QUOTE: "[Their words about working with you]" — [First name, Title]
```

Example:
> A 12-person HR consulting firm was spending 15 hours per week manually compiling client reports. We built an AI workflow that pulls from their existing tools and produces a draft report in under 10 minutes. They got those 15 hours back in the first week — and the team called it "the best thing we've done this year."

**The Referral Ask (timing matters):**

Ask at the moment of peak satisfaction — usually right after they see the first real result, not at project close.

Script:
> "I'm so glad that's working for you. I work almost entirely through referrals — is there anyone in your network who you think is dealing with a similar challenge? I'd love an introduction."

Then stop talking. Let them think.

**The Upsell Path — Build It Before You Need It:**

| Tier 1 completed | Natural Tier 2 |
|---|---|
| AI audit / diagnostic | Implementation project |
| One-time workflow build | Monthly maintenance retainer |
| Strategy engagement | "Done with you" execution support |
| Training / workshop | Ongoing coaching retainer |

Before your project ends, plant the seed:
> "We're almost done with Phase 1. A lot of clients at this point want help with [logical next thing]. Is that something you'd want to explore after this wraps?"

## The Master Sequence — Where to Start

If you're brand new, do these in this order:

1. **Items 2 + 3** — Niche and ICP first. Everything else flows from this.
2. **Item 1** — Validate with 10 conversations before building anything.
3. **Item 10** — Define your offer so you can describe it when you talk to people.
4. **Item 6** — Start warm outreach immediately.
5. **Item 5** — Have your pricing ready before the first sales conversation.
6. **Item 4** — Business structure (can run parallel to 2–5).
7. **Items 7 + 8** — Delivery system and legal basics (before you sign your first client).
8. **Item 9** — Minimal operations stack (just the 4 core tools).
9. **Item 11** — Start promoting from day one, even while doing 1–8.
10. **Items 12 + 13** — Client management and growth systems (as you start working with clients).
