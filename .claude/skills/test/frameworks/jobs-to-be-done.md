# Framework: Jobs to Be Done (Clayton Christensen)

**Use when:** the user already has a customer in mind and has talked to real people — they have signal, they just need to sharpen the *job* that customer is hiring this product/service to do.

**The idea (teach it in one line):** People don't buy products — they hire them to make progress on a job in their life. Find the job, and the product, the pitch, and the price all get easier.

## Question flow

Ask one at a time. After each answer, reflect it back in one line before asking the next.

### Q1 — Who are you solving for?
> Picture the specific person you're building this for. Tell me about them — their role, what their day looks like, what they spend most of their time on.

### Q2 — The moment of frustration
> Tell me about the moment this frustrates them — what are they trying to do right before they'd think "I need something better than this"?

### Q3 — What they hire today
> What do they currently "hire" to get this job done — even if it's a workaround, a spreadsheet, a competitor, or doing nothing? What does that cost them in time, money, or stress?

### Q4 — What "solved" makes possible
> If this job were done perfectly, what would change for them? Think practically (what gets done), emotionally (how they'd feel), and socially (how they'd look to others).

After Q4, hand back to the main skill for synthesis.
