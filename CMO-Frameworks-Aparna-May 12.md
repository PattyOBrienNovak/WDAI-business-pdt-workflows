## What the CMO Owns

The CMO translates the CEO's strategic bets into language clients understand, gets remembered in a noisy market, and builds the credibility ladder that turns strangers into believers. For an AI business in 2026, the CMO's hardest job is being recognized as different when 67% of software websites mention AI.

## The Recommended Evergreen Frameworks

### 1. Obviously Awesome — The 10-Step Positioning Process ★ (Backbone)

**Source:** April Dunford, *Obviously Awesome* (2019). Dunford spent 25 years positioning B2B tech products; this is widely considered THE founder-era positioning playbook.

**What it is:** Ten steps that move from a confused "I do AI stuff" to a sharp "obviously the best for this specific job":

1. Understand the customers who love your product
2. Form a positioning team
3. Align positioning vocabulary; let go of baggage
4. List your true competitive alternatives
5. Isolate your unique attributes
6. Map attributes to value themes
7. Determine who cares a lot
8. Find a market frame of reference that puts your strengths at the center
9. Layer on a trend (carefully)
10. Capture positioning so it can be shared

**Why it fits:** Dunford's key insight — *your positioning is determined by your competitive alternatives, not your features* — is the antidote to the WDAI member who lists their AI tools instead of explaining their value. It also gives explicit permission to layer on the AI trend (Step 9) without leading with it.

**How it powers a command:** `/cmo-positioning` walks all 10 steps. Output is a one-page Positioning Statement using Dunford's template. Upgrades Patty's 4-Layer Positioning Stack — Patty's stack becomes the "quick start" version; Dunford is the deep version.

### 2. StoryBrand SB7 — The BrandScript ★ (Messaging framework)

**Source:** Donald Miller, *Building a StoryBrand* (2017). The most influential small-business marketing framework of the last decade. Already mentioned in our Phase-1-Consolidated research but not yet operationalized.

**What it is:** Seven story elements that turn marketing copy from "look at us" to "here's how we'll help you win":

1. **A Character** (the client, not you) — what do they want?
2. **Has a problem** — external, internal, philosophical
3. **And meets a guide** (you) — who shows empathy and authority
4. **Who gives them a plan** — a simple 3-step path
5. **And calls them to action** — direct + transitional CTAs
6. **That helps them avoid failure** — what's at stake
7. **And ends in success** — what life looks like after

**Why it fits:** WDAI members come from accomplished backgrounds and instinctively lead with credentials ("20 years in HR before AI"). StoryBrand's core rule — **the client is the hero, you are the guide** — is the single most useful reframe for this audience. It also produces copy that fits in a bio, landing page, pitch deck, and email template.

**How it powers a command:** `/cmo-messaging` walks the 7 BrandScript elements and outputs a one-page BrandScript. Every other marketing artifact (LinkedIn bio, landing page, email signature, pitch) is then derived from this single document.

### 3. How Brands Grow — Mental Availability + Distinctive Brand Assets

**Source:** Byron Sharp, *How Brands Grow* (Ehrenberg-Bass Institute, 2010). The most empirically-grounded brand growth book ever written — based on 50+ years of buyer behavior data across thousands of brands.

**What it is:** Two big ideas that quietly destroy most "small brand" advice:

- **Mental availability** — the probability your brand comes to mind when a buying situation arises. Sharp's research shows this explains 60-70% of market share variance. Translation: **getting remembered beats getting loved.**
- **Distinctive Brand Assets (DBAs)** — the consistent visual + verbal mnemonics (color, name, font, tagline, character, sound) that let buyers recognize you across contexts. Solo founders almost never invest in these.

**Why it fits:** Most small-business marketing advice tells founders to "build a passionate community" and "delight your customers." Sharp's data says: that's largely a myth. What actually works is **reach + memorability + consistency**. For a solo AI founder, this is liberating — you don't need to make people love you, you need to be the first name they think of when their pain hits.

**How it powers a command:** `/cmo-brandidentity` produces the founder's Distinctive Brand Asset inventory — name, tagline, color palette direction, signature phrase, recurring content format. Outputs a one-page "Brand Identity Brief" that constrains every downstream artifact for consistency. This sits alongside the CDO/Design workflow but is *upstream* of it — the CMO defines the assets, design executes them.

**Note on naming boundary with CDO:** `/cmo-brandidentity` (CMO) *defines* the verbal/visual identity at the strategic level — name, tagline, voice, color direction, signature phrase. `/design-brand` (CDO) *executes* it — full visual system, design tokens, typography, components. This is how it works at real companies: CMO owns brand strategy, CDO owns brand execution.

### 4. Cialdini's Six Principles — Applied to the Credibility Stack ★ (Credibility framework)

**Source:** Robert Cialdini, *Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion* (1984, updated 2021). HBR's "Harnessing the Science of Persuasion" (October 2001) remains one of HBR's most-cited articles on influence. 40+ years of replication. The Phase 1 brainstorm explicitly flagged credibility as a gap — this is the framework that fills it.

**What it is:** Six universal levers of influence. For the credibility piece specifically, three matter most:

- **Authority** — credentials, expertise signals, published work, named clients
- **Social Proof** — testimonials, case studies, "X clients served," media mentions
- **Liking** — building genuine human connection (similarity, compliments, cooperation)

The other three (reciprocity, commitment/consistency, scarcity) come into play in pricing and sales — better suited to the CRO workflow.

**Why it fits:** Phase 1 says "offer free work in exchange for testimonials" — this is exactly Cialdini's Social Proof + Reciprocity in action. But "free work" is just one tactic. Cialdini gives the **full menu** of credibility moves, so a founder without testimonials yet can still build authority (publishing) and liking (community presence) in parallel.

**How it powers a command:** `/cmo-credibility` audits which Cialdini levers the founder currently has, which are weak, and prescribes the next move. Output: a "Credibility Stack" doc — Authority signals, Social Proof signals, Liking signals — with the gaps explicitly named and a 30/60/90-day plan to fill them.

### 5. Permission Marketing — Build the Audience That Lets You In

**Source:** Seth Godin, *Permission Marketing* (1999). Still one of the most prescient marketing books of the modern era — Godin predicted the email/newsletter economy a decade before it happened.

**What it is:** Marketing earns its right to interrupt. Don't shout at strangers — turn strangers into *friends* (people who give you permission to contact them) and friends into *customers*. The asset is the list of people who've said "yes, I want to hear from you."

**Why it fits:** The Phase 1 research notes "One platform, consistently: 3-5x per week for 90 days." That's necessary but not sufficient — posting builds reach, but it doesn't build a list. **Permission Marketing fixes the missing piece**: posts → newsletter signups → email subscribers → eventual customers. For a solo founder, an owned email list of 500 right-fit subscribers is worth more than 50,000 LinkedIn followers.

**How it powers a command:** `/cmo-audience` designs the founder's "Permission Asset" — what they're going to publish regularly that gives people a reason to subscribe (newsletter, podcast, framework drops). Output: a permission strategy doc — what's the freebie/lead magnet, what's the cadence, where does signup live, what does the welcome sequence say.

### 6. The 1-to-5 Content Engine + 90-Day Consistency Plan

**Source:** Patty's Phase-1-Frameworks-Patty.md (Item 11). Already documented in the repo — keeping it because it's the right executional model and was researched against real consultant practice.

**What it is:** One source event (client win, lesson learned, recurring question) → five content artifacts (LinkedIn post, carousel/thread, newsletter section, short video, reusable framework). Run on a 90-day consistency plan: weeks 1-4 = show up + share learnings; weeks 5-8 = share client work; weeks 9-12 = introduce offer with direct CTAs.

**Why it fits:** This is the daily execution layer. Dunford gives you what to say; Miller gives you how to frame it; Sharp tells you to be consistent; Godin tells you to build the list; Cialdini tells you what evidence to attach. **Patty's framework is how you operationalize all of the above week after week.** It also pairs perfectly with the existing "Building in Public" formula she wrote.

**How it powers a command:** `/cmo-content` runs each source event through the 1-to-5 generator and outputs the 5 derived artifacts with channel-specific framing. Also tracks consistency against the 90-day plan.

## What's Intentionally Left Out (And Why)

| Framework | Why we're skipping it |
|---|---|
| **Kotler's 4 Ps / 7 Ps** | Foundational, but too abstract for solo founders. Useful in an MBA classroom, not in a Build Hour. |
| **AIDA (Attention/Interest/Desire/Action)** | Showing its age. StoryBrand is the modern, more usable replacement. |
| **Crossing the Chasm (Geoffrey Moore)** | Brilliant book, but designed for tech startups scaling past the early adopter cliff — most WDAI members aren't there yet. Add later as an advanced module (and may live in a GTM convergence workflow, not under CMO). |
| **Inbound Marketing (HubSpot's model)** | Mostly subsumed by Permission Marketing + content engine; HubSpot's framework is more about funnel mechanics than founder positioning. |
| **Customer Journey Mapping** | Useful diagnostic, but redundant once StoryBrand + Permission are in place. |
| **GTM frameworks (Bullseye, AARRR)** | GTM is a cross-functional convergence point (CEO + CMO + CRO + CPO), not a CMO sub-command. Belongs in a separate `/launch` convergence workflow. |

## How These Frameworks Connect

```
   POSITIONING (Dunford — the bet — who you're FOR)
        ↓
   MESSAGING (StoryBrand — how you SAY it)
        ↓
   BRAND IDENTITY (Sharp DBAs — how you're REMEMBERED)
        ↓
   CREDIBILITY (Cialdini — why you're TRUSTED)
        ↓
   AUDIENCE (Godin Permission — who's LISTENING)
        ↓
   CONTENT (1-to-5 Engine — how you SHOW UP weekly)
```

Each layer feeds the next. Position the bet → tell the story → make it memorable → back it with credibility → build the audience → show up consistently. Skip a layer and the system breaks.

## The 6 CMO Sub-Commands

Named after the question a total beginner asks themselves at each step — so the sequence flows naturally without needing marketing jargon:

| Command | Beginner's Question | Framework | What it does |
|---|---|---|---|
| **`/cmo-positioning`** | "Who is this for, and why am I better than the alternatives?" | Dunford 10-Step | Walks the founder through Dunford's positioning process. Output: a one-page Positioning Statement (target customer, competitive alternatives, unique attributes, value themes, market frame, trend layer). Upgrades Patty's 4-Layer Stack. Inherits ICP + JTBD from CEO workflow. |
| **`/cmo-messaging`** | "What do I actually say about what I do?" | StoryBrand SB7 | Walks the 7 BrandScript elements. Output: a one-page BrandScript that becomes the source for LinkedIn bio, landing page hero, pitch deck cover, email signature, elevator pitch. |
| **`/cmo-brandidentity`** | "How will people recognize and remember me?" | Byron Sharp DBAs | Defines the founder's Distinctive Brand Assets — name, tagline, color, signature phrase, recurring format. Output: a one-page Brand Identity Brief that constrains all downstream visual/verbal work and hands cleanly to the CDO workflow. |
| **`/cmo-credibility`** | "Why should anyone believe me yet?" | Cialdini (Authority/Social Proof/Liking) | Audits current credibility evidence, names gaps, prescribes next move. Output: a Credibility Stack doc + 30/60/90-day plan to fill the strongest gap. |
| **`/cmo-audience`** | "How do I get people who actually want to hear from me?" | Godin Permission Marketing | Designs the founder's Permission Asset (newsletter, lead magnet, signup mechanism, welcome sequence). Output: a Permission Strategy doc — what to publish, where signup lives, first 5 welcome emails. |
| **`/cmo-content`** | "How do I keep showing up every week without burning out?" | Patty's 1-to-5 Engine + 90-Day Plan | Runs source events through the 1-to-5 generator. Output: 5 channel-specific artifacts per source event + a consistency tracker against the 90-day plan. |

**The beginner sequence reads as a sentence:** *Position the offer → craft the message → build the brand identity → earn credibility → grow the audience → publish content consistently.*

## Connection to Other C-Suite Workflows

The CMO inherits *from* and feeds *into* the rest of the team:

- **CEO → CMO:** JTBD, ICP, and strategic choices (Playing to Win) become positioning inputs. The CEO's Hedgehog defines what the CMO is allowed to claim.
- **CMO → CRO:** Positioning and messaging frame the sales conversation. The BrandScript's "Call to Action" element directly seeds the CRO's pitch.
- **CMO → CDO:** Brand Identity Brief is the input to `/design-brand`. The CMO defines the assets; design executes them visually.
- **CMO → CPO:** The "who cares a lot" output from positioning Step 7 validates or challenges CPO's user personas.
- **CMO ↔ COO:** Permission Marketing assets (email list, newsletter) need ops infrastructure — convergence point.

## A Note on GTM (Go-to-Market)

GTM frameworks (Bullseye, AARRR, Crossing the Chasm) **do not** live inside the CMO role. GTM is a cross-functional convergence point — it requires CEO (segment choice), CMO (positioning + message), CRO (pricing + sales motion), and CPO (product readiness) to all sync before launch.

The May 5 brainstorm already identified "Go-to-market" as one of the four convergence points. The recommendation is to build a **top-level `/launch` convergence command** that orchestrates inputs from each role and outputs a GTM Plan + Launch Readiness Checklist. It would draw from Bullseye (channel selection) and AARRR (metrics) as its core GTM frameworks.

This boundary keeps CMO scoped to what the CMO actually controls and prevents GTM decisions from being made in a marketing silo.

## Triggers and Trigger Re-Entry

The May 5 brainstorm introduced the iterative trigger model — the same workflow re-fires when specific events happen. Here's how the CMO workflow re-fires:

### When the FULL CMO sequence runs

| Trigger | What just happened | Why all 6 commands re-fire |
|---|---|---|
| **New startup** | Founder is starting from zero | Need to define everything from positioning to content cadence |
| **Major pivot** | CEO declared a pivot via `/ceo-pivot` | Old positioning is dead — full reset required |
| **New market entry** | Expanding to a second niche or geography | Each market needs its own positioning, messaging, audience |

### When a SUBSET re-fires (partial triggers)

| Trigger | What just happened | Commands that re-fire | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Lost a deal to a specific competitor** | Heard "we went with X instead" 2+ times | `/cmo-positioning` → `/cmo-messaging` | Competitive frame has shifted — positioning may be misaligned |
| **Launching a new offer or package** | New service tier, new productized offer | `/cmo-messaging` (offer-specific) + `/cmo-content` (announcement series) | Need new BrandScript for this offer + content rollout |
| **First case study lands** | Completed first project with a quotable result | `/cmo-credibility` (update the stack) + `/cmo-content` (1-to-5 from the win) | New evidence to harvest, new content fuel |
| **Audience growth plateau** | List/followers haven't grown in 30+ days | `/cmo-brandidentity` (audit recognition) + `/cmo-audience` (audit list mechanics) | Either memorability or permission asset is broken |
| **Engagement collapse** | Post engagement down 50%+ for 2+ weeks | `/cmo-messaging` (refresh BrandScript) + `/cmo-content` (mix change) | Message has gone stale or content format has fatigued |
| **Quarterly review** | End of 90-day content plan | `/cmo-content` (consistency check) + `/cmo-credibility` (gap audit) | Periodic health check — natural cadence |
| **CEO assumptions broke** | `/ceo-assumptions` flagged a stale assumption about the market or customer | `/cmo-positioning` (re-test) | Positioning may rest on the broken assumption |
| **Brand inconsistency surfaced** | Designer, client, or community pointed out off-brand work | `/cmo-brandidentity` (re-anchor DBAs) | Need to re-establish the visual/verbal mnemonics |
| **Hiring a designer / agency** | About to bring in external creative help | `/cmo-brandidentity` (output is the brief they need) | The Brand Identity Brief is the handoff doc |

### Convergence triggers (CMO + other roles)

| Trigger | Commands that fire across roles |
|---|---|
| **Go-to-market launch** | `/cmo-positioning` + `/cmo-messaging` + CRO pricing + CPO readiness → handoff to `/launch` convergence command |
| **First client signed** | `/cmo-content` (announce the win) + CRO `/cro-onboard` + COO `/coo-deliver` |
| **Pivot decision** | `/ceo-pivot` → `/cmo-positioning` (full reset) → `/cro-price` (re-anchor) |
